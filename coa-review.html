How the decision is made. The lab's own Pass/Fail remarks are informational only — this page independently resolves the applicable limit from the embedded reference (US_Gasoline_RVP_Reference_v3: summer EPA limits by state, RFG 7.4 areas, 7.8-psi areas, SIP areas, and the ASTM D4814-24a monthly and class schedules) and evaluates every reported DVPE and D86 T50 against it: blendstock samples against the blendstock limit, the E10 hand blend against the finished-gasoline limit. The evaluation basis is a target volatility class (AAA–E, the default) for product blended ahead of the next period, or the month of sale (as-tested period) — class-based approvals carry a sale release date: immediate if the current period already permits the class (RVP and T50 window), otherwise the first date the location's schedule opens to it, and the product is held until then rather than failed. The lab's Pass/Fail remarks never block in either basis — they are carried as advisories, and the decision comes solely from this page's internal logic. An optional test-method precision allowance (default 40% of reproducibility) releases results marginally past the nominal limit, flagged distinctly on the record: RVP uses D5191 R = 0.0161 × (DVPE + 18.0) psi; T50 uses the slope-dependent R = 4.65 + 1.20 × slope °F with slope = (T60−T40)/20 from the reported T40/T60, or the 3.40°F/% workbook-average slope when those are not reported (coefficients from Test_Method_Precisions.xlsx, pending confirmation against the current D5191 / D86 editions). All samples within (effective) limits → APPROVED — for immediate sale or with a release date — with a printable approval record; any exceedance beyond the allowance, missing result, or unresolvable limit → SENT FOR REVIEW to Quality Control (K. Stocksen) with the reasons listed — only internal-logic failures route to review. When the lab's stated max or remark differs from the internal determination, the discrepancy is flagged even when the result still passes. The 2026 EPA national fuel waiver toggle appears only when a summer month or a summer class (≤9.0 psi) is selected. Extraction is never trusted blindly — a person verifies the grid before evaluation, and results provenance (parsed vs manual) is recorded.
